How to Build a Tiramisu

Ingredients

  • 3 Eggs
  • 1/2 Cup Sugar
  • 8 Oz. Mascarpone
  • ~1 Cup Strong Coffee
  • 2 Tbps cognac
  • Cocoa Powder
  • Ground Coffee
  • 20 Ladyfingers
Hide Images

Preparation

The eggs and mascarpone should be room temperature, so leave them outside the fridge for an hour before you start. The coffee can be prepared beforehand as well.

before blending in the mascarpone

Part 1 - The Egg Yolks Bowl

Seperate the egg yolks from the whites and put them into different bowls. Keep the yolks, any fatty materials, or other impurities out of the egg whites bowl. Add the sugar to the egg yolks and blend well. Add the mascarpone and blend well, and then blend some more. Finally, add the cognac and blend just a bit more.

whipped egg whites sufficiently stiff

Part 2 - Whipping the Egg Whites

Warm up the egg whites by filling another bowl with hot water and floating the bowl wth the egg whites inside it for a few minutes. Clean your blender blades well. Add a pinch of sugar to the egg whites and then whip them until they are solid enough that stiff peaks of whipped egg whites form if you move a fork through them.

tiramisu mixture ready to go!

Part 3 - Coalescence

With a spoon, scoop the egg whites into the egg yolks mixture. Don't use a blender, as using a blender or otherwise mixing violently at this point will negatively affect the texture of the whipped egg whites. Using a spoon, scoop from the bottom of the bowl to the top in a circular motion until the mixture is consistent.

building a second layer of lady fingers atop the first

Part 4 - Building the Tiramisu

The ideal size of dish for this tiramisu would fit half your lady fingers in a single layer, about a foot by a foot. Pour the coffee into another small, flat dish. Assemble your first layer of lady fingers by dipping them in the coffee and putting them in your dish. Pour a third of the mixture over them, and sprinkle a small amount of the cocoa powder and ground coffee over the mixture. Build another layer of lady fingers, also dipped in coffee, on top of the first. Pour the rest of the mixture on top. Cover with a fine and consistent layer of cocoa powder. You want just enough cocoa so that the white mixture is not visible underneath. Sprinkle with ground coffee, cover with something airtight, and refrigerate for at least 3 hours before serving.
